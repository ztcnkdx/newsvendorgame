Supply Chain Game: The Contract Designer (Zamatia-UV)

Case Description

Zamatia, Ltd. manufactures upscale Italian sunglasses and sells them to Umbra Visage (UV), a Miami-based retailer operating boutique stores.

Replenishment lead time is long and the selling season is short, so UV receives only one shipment of Zamatia sunglasses each season.

UV's excess inventory at the end of the selling season can be bought back by Zamatia at a pre-negotiated buy-back price.

Game Parameters

You are Zamatia (Manufacturer). You set the Wholesale Price (W) and Buy-Back Price (B).

UV (Retailer) is rational and will always order the quantity (Q*) that maximizes their own profit.

Analysis: Critical Ratios

Coordination is achieved when the Retailer's Critical Ratio (the optimal service level) matches the Supply Chain's Critical Ratio. The Buy-Back price (B) reduces the retailer's cost of overage, increasing their Critical Ratio.

Level 3: Profit Splitting (Pareto Improvement)

The total profit "pie" is now maximized. In this level, you control the Wholesale Price (W). The system automatically calculates the required Buy-Back Price (B) to maintain coordination.
